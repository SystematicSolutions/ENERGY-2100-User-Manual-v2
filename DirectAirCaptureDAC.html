Direct Air Capture (DAC)

Enabling Direct Air Capture

Direct Air Capture (DAC) is a module in the model that contains code to simulate energy consumption, emissions, and carbon sequestration of direct air capture devices for each modeled year. Demand and emission values from this module can be found in the DirectAirCapture economic sector in the model results.  Reduction in carbon emissions from running devices can be also be seen in the overall GHG inventory totals. 

The DAC module requires inputs to produce an impact in the model results. These inputs can vary depending on which option (DACSw) is selected for simulating DAC in the model. Below is a list of options and the inputs required for each:

  • Exogenous DAC (DACSw = 0) 
    • xDACDem[Area,Year]  - Exogenous demand for DAC 
  • DAC used to meet emissions goal (DACSw = 1)
    • xPolGoal[Area,Year] – Emissions goal
  • DAC used to meet emissions goal or produce to an exogenous level (whichever is higher) (DACSw = 2)
    • xDACDem[Area,Year]  - Exogenous demand for DAC 
    • xPolGoal[Area,Year] – Emissions goal
  • DAC meets national Goal, with production split by exogenous weights (DACSw = 3)
    • DACDemNation[Nation,Year] – National DAC demands
    • xGoalPolNation[Nation,Year] – National emissions goal
    • DACWeight[Area,Year] - Share of area DAC demands of national total
  • DAC response to Carbon Price (DACSw = 4)
    • eCO2Price[Area,Year] – Carbon price
    • DACWeight[Area,Year] - Share of area DAC demands of national total
    • DACPriceDiffMax[Area,Year] – Price difference fraction for building DAC
